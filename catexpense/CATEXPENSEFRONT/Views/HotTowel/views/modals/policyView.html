Employees should first work with Laura DiValerio regarding car rental arrangements.

If this is not possible and an Employee must make his or her own car rental arrangements, the following rules apply.

Employees must use the most cost-effective means of ground transportation.

Employees must rent compact or intermediate size cars unless approved in advance by the Employee’s supervisor.

Employees must decline all fuel options and return the car with a full tank of gas.

Employees should rent the car in the name of Catalyst IT Services, Inc. and decline all insurance options as Catalyst has an automobile liability insurance policy for cars rented by employees that are used for business purposes and are rented in the name of the company.

a. Employees’ Vehicles.

An Employee will be reimbursed for use of the Employee’s vehicles used within the Employee’s scope of work (other than transportation between home and the Employee’s regular worksite).

Mileage detail must be listed on the Employee’s expense report.

An Employee who is traveling to a client site rather than the office should deduct/not count mileage the Employee would normally spend commuting to the office.

b. Airport Parking.

Employees should use long-term parking unless the trip will be for two days or less, in which case Employees may use daily parking.

c. Use of Taxi Service.

The daily expense for taxi service should not exceed the cost of a car rental.

Employees should first work with Laura DiValerio regarding lodging arrangements.

If this is not possible and an Employee must make his or her own lodging arrangements, the following rules apply.

If an Employee’s scope of work requires the use of overnight lodging, the Employee should use moderately-priced single room accommodations.

It is the Employee’s responsibility to cancel any reservation in a timely fashion in order to avoid a "No Show" charge.

Employees will not be reimbursed for avoidable \"No Show\" charges.

Employees may not make lodging selections based on frequent traveler programs.

Employees should use long-term parking unless the trip will be for two days or less, in which case Employees may use daily parking.

The following is a partial list of items that Catalyst will not reimburse:

• Transportation between home and regular worksite

• Traffic or parking tickets

• Frequent traveler program fees

• Additional travel or car rental insurance costs

• Navigational systems in rental cars

• Credit card and other late fees

• Airline club membership fees

• In-room movies

• Personal items lost, stolen or damaged while traveling.

Employees should first work with Laura DiValerio regarding air and train travel arrangements.

If this is not possible and an Employee must make his or her own air or train arrangements, the following rules apply:

a. Lowest Logical Fare.

Employees should obtain the lowest logical fare when traveling by commercial airline or train.

In the case of air travel, the lowest logical rate may require the Employee to use alternative (e.g. regional discount) carriers or secondary airport.

By saying “lowest logical fare,” Catalyst means for its Employees to use reasonable discretion.

For instance, it is not necessary to take an overnight trip solely to achieve a lower fare (and, when combined with the cost of lodging, it may not lead to savings in any event).

Similarly, if a higher fare will save hours’ worth of travel time, it is reasonable to choose the higher fare.

b. Class of Service.

Employees must travel in coach class.

Employees may upgrade airline or train tickets only at their own expense.

The one exception to the foregoing is that if an Employee is traveling via Amtrak and coach is oversold, he or she may upgrade to business class for that leg.

c. Frequent Flyer Programs.

Mileage credits and other benefits from individual frequent traveler programs may be accumulated by Employees for personal use; however, Employees may not make travel selections based on frequent traveler programs.

• Additional travel or car rental insurance costs

• Navigational systems in rental cars

• Credit card and other late fees

• Airline club membership fees
